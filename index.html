Our Speciality

Experience authentic Gujrati flavors in all our dishes with best quality and hygiene.

Surati Khaman

It is also Called as VATI DAL NA KHAMAN (In Gujarat) Vaati Daal Na Khaman is distinct from the Nylon Khaman in the sense that they are made of Chana Daal.

Nylon Khaman

These Khaman are so soft and spongy that the reference to nylon is really apt! As they are also known as Regular Khaman (Made up of Besan)

Shahi Handvo

Handvo is a vegetable cake originating from the gujarat, india. It is made with a bottle gourd filling, also many other vegetables are added. It is often eaten with green chutney or tomato ketchup.

Fafda

Fafda is a fried crispy crunchy tasty snack made with besan, laced with carom seeds and black pepper.

Khandvi

Savory snack of Gujrati cusine, tightyl rolled bite size pieces primarily made of gram flour and yogurt.

Thepla

Thepla is a spiced flavorful flatbread made of whole wheat flour, oil and spices . Typically enjoyed with pickle or yougart.

Chakli

Crisp & crunchy, chakli is a spiral shaped deep-fried snack made from wheat flour.

Dhokla

Khatta Dhokla or dhokla is savory snack made of rice and Urad Daal.

Muthiya

Muthiya is a Gujrati delicacy made with bottle guard and fenugreek. This dish can be eaten steamed or fried (after steaming); it is equally tasty either way.

Sandwich Khaman

Layer of mint chutney sandwiched between nylon khaman and dhokla. A unique amalgamation to give you the best of both worlds.

Shahi Patra

A tasty gujrati made of arbi k patte (colocasia leaves) rolled with spiced and flavourful gram batter.

Shahi Undhiyu

Undhiyu is a full of flavor mixed vegetable curry from the State of Gujarat. Traditionally cooked, undhiyu is a must try with puri when it comes to Gujarati cuisine

Sev Khamani [Amiri Khaman]

Sev khamani is traditionally made from the surati khaman & also known as amiri khaman. It is tempered with garlic & mixed with pomegranate seeds & coconut.

About Us

In 1966, my grandfather Mr. Mathuradas Ganatra moved to Indore with his family and opened a general store for a living but it didn't work for them. As you have heard you can move out of Gujrat but cannot take Gujrat out from you, my grandmother used to make amazing khaman at home. One of our relative gave 300 rs to my grandfather and insisted him to start the selling khaman. They bought the raw materials and my grandmother made khaman at night. The next day my grandfather went to the market to sell it. He went on a cycle to every small and large sweet and namkeen shops to distribute khaman samples. As Indore was famous for its poha jalebi no one was accepting a Gujrati dish as it was very new for them. But my grandfather convinced them to take samples and promised them that he will take them back if they didn't sell. Within 3 hours my grandfather got to know all the samples were sold and people are asking for more. It was 1980 and from there, our journey began and Shahi Khaman started getting recognition. Who would have thought a place where people die for poha will accept and show this amount of love to khaman.
